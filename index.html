Circus Arts

I am a circus performer and one part of Duo Sadboi and Trio SadKittens.

Duo Sadboi Bio:

Dylan Corvidae and Bentley Moses (Duo Sadboi), are queer circus performers whose gender-defying duo acts showcase the power of trust, vulnerability, and interpersonal connection. Duo Sadboi, established in 2017, currently specializes in dance trapeze -- through their work together, Dylan and Bentley invite the audience to appreciate not only the spectacle they are seeing, but to imagine themselves in the place of each character portrayed. In addition to their Duo Sadboi projects, Dylan and Bentley's skills encompass a variety of circus arts as diverse as their multifaceted identities. Independently, Dylan trains in hand balancing, tumbling, yoga, various forms of dance, and is a multi-instrumentalist/vocalist; Bentley collaborates on duo projects on lyra, trapeze, and chair dance and is an aerial coach at Night Flight Aerial. Together, Dylan and Bentley are members of Sir Cupcake's Queer Circus (led by Jack Stocklynn).

Music

Music has always been a huge part of my identity. I was exposed to live music at a very early age in the form of barn dances and Irish Ceilis. My own musical pursuits started in elementary school with the trumpet. In high school I picked up a guitar and never looked back. I started composing electronic music in college and started learning piano then. I can play bass, but I play bass like a guitarist. I love to experiment with sound, hence I own many effects pedals. I play guitar with a cello bow, ebow, slide, trem picking, tapping, drumsticks, whatever I can think of I try. Life and art are about creation, experimentation, and pushing boundaries. I love epic romantic music that builds atmospheres and evokes feelings through sound without use of words.

Genres of music I enjoy: Post-Metal, Death, Doom and Black Metal, Post-Hardcore, Hardcore, Cascadian Black and Doom Metal, Blackgaze, Dream Thrash, Punk, Post-punk, Swedish Skreamo, Celtic/Irish folk, Fast Dirty Bluegrass, film and tv scoring.

Music is very important to me and has shaped me into who I am today. More specifically the hardcore punk and metal scene. A lot of the ideals that I hold dear have come from what that scene exposed me to and it will always be my home. Some of the things I learned in the heavy music community were the ideas of making my own family of people who I'm not related to but can depend on and love (intentional family), my love of socialism and progressive causes, respect for the self and body, striving to be a better person in spite of the world we live in and the horrible things people do to each other and themselves, and having passion for life and love.
